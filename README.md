This is a concept of a registry that operates as an independent, publicly accessible record of AI-related patient safety incidents in UK healthcare. It complements — not replaces — LFPSE, MHRA Yellow Card, and trust-level incident reporting.

Its purpose is to create a shared, navigable evidence base that clinical safety authors, procurement leads, CNIO and CCIO teams, regulators, and patients can consult when assessing the real-world safety profile of healthcare AI tools. Pre-deployment hazard analyses conducted without post-deployment evidence represent a structural patient safety risk.

The registry accepts: (a) patient harms where AI was a direct or proximate cause; (b) near misses prevented by clinical vigilance or safety netting; (c) systemic risks or patterns not yet linked to a specific harm event. Reports are accepted from clinicians, digital leads, patient safety officers, and patients or carers.

Each submission is reviewed by an independent clinical safety review panel before publication. Identifiable information — patient details, staff names, trust names — is removed prior to public display. Reports are assigned a formal UKHAI reference number. The review panel includes a registered mental health nurse, a clinical safety officer, a lay patient representative, and an independent clinical informaticist.

This registry does not replace LFPSE (NHS England), the MHRA Yellow Card AI pathway, or manufacturer post-market surveillance obligations under the Medical Devices Regulations 2025. It aggregates and synthesises public-interest learning, and accepts direct reports where existing mechanisms do not capture AI-specific causation.
The registry operates in alignment with the Health and Social Care (Safety and Quality) Act 2015 and the protected disclosure model in NHS PSIRF. Reports submitted in good faith are handled under safe harbour principles equivalent to those governing NHS serious incident reports.

Designed in alignment with the UNESCO Recommendation on the Ethics of Artificial Intelligence (2021), the EU AI Act Annex III high-risk provisions, and the AHRQ AI in Healthcare Safety Program (USA). Supports the work of the UK National Commission on the Regulation of AI in Healthcare (2025–26), whose recommendations are due summer 2026.

Concept design originated following national discussion on the absence of UK healthcare AI harm reporting infrastructure (May 2026). Registry design informed by DCB0129 clinical safety officer practice, LFPSE taxonomy, MHRA Yellow Card scheme, AHRQ AI safety programme, and UNESCO Recommendation on the Ethics of Artificial Intelligence (2021).
